SDG 11: Sustainable Cities & Communities

Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.

Explore Targets

The Urban Challenge

Over half of the world's population now lives in urban areas, a figure expected to rise to two-thirds by 2050. This rapid urbanization, while a catalyst for economic growth, presents enormous challenges: slums, inadequate infrastructure, rising pollution, and lack of affordable housing.

SDG 11 aims to transform how we build and manage urban spaces, ensuring that cities are not only functional but also equitable, resilient to climate change, and environmentally friendly.

Key Targets for 2030

Target 11.1

Affordable Housing & Slum Upgrading

Ensure access for all to adequate, safe, and affordable housing and basic services, and upgrade slums.

Target 11.2

Sustainable Transport

Provide access to safe, affordable, accessible, and sustainable transport systems for all, improving road safety.

Target 11.3

Inclusive Urbanization

Enhance inclusive and sustainable urbanization and capacity for participatory, integrated human settlement planning.

Target 11.4

Protecting Heritage

Strengthen efforts to protect and safeguard the world’s cultural and natural heritage.

Target 11.5

Disaster Reduction

Significantly reduce the number of deaths and people affected by disasters, focusing on the poor and those in vulnerable situations.

Target 11.6

Reducing Pollution

Reduce the adverse per capita environmental impact of cities, including by paying special attention to air quality and waste management.

How We Achieve Urban Sustainability

Public and Green Spaces

Developing and protecting accessible, safe, and inclusive green public spaces, particularly for women, children, older persons, and persons with disabilities. This improves social connection and mental health.

Climate Change Adaptation

Implementing integrated policies towards climate change mitigation and adaptation, disaster risk reduction, and holistic management of natural resources, including water and biodiversity.

Smart Infrastructure

Leveraging technology for better urban management—smart grids, IoT sensors for traffic and waste, and digital platforms to enhance citizen participation in city governance.

Did you know? By 2030, slum dwellers worldwide are expected to grow to 2 billion unless drastic action is taken on Target 11.1.
