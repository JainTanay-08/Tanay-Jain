Closing the Loop: Circular Economy & Water Resilience

Moving away from a "take-make-dispose" mentality requires innovative systems for waste minimization and water cycle management.

From Waste to Resource

The goal is to eliminate waste by design. Smart bins report fill levels to optimize collection routes, and specialized facilities convert organic waste into biofuel and compost.

  • Prioritizing repair and refurbishment over replacement.
  • Advanced material recovery facilities (MRF) for high-grade recycling.
  • Incentivizing producers to take responsibility for end-of-life products.

Rainwater & Greywater Recycling

Minimizing fresh water consumption through smart leak detection, low-flow fixtures, and treating non-potable greywater for uses like irrigation and toilet flushing.

  • Green infrastructure (permeable pavements) to reduce stormwater runoff.
  • Decentralized treatment plants for local water management.
  • Water pricing structured to incentivize conservation.
